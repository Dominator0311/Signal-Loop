# NICE NG203 — Chronic Kidney Disease: Assessment and Management

Source: National Institute for Health and Care Excellence (2021, updated 2024)
URL: https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng203

## Key Recommendations for Prescribing in CKD

### 1.3 Pharmacological Management

**NSAIDs and nephrotoxic drugs:**
- In adults with CKD, chronic use of NSAIDs may be associated with progression and acute use is associated with a reversible decrease in GFR.
- Exercise caution when giving NSAIDs to people with CKD over prolonged periods of time.
- Monitor the effects on GFR, particularly in people with a low baseline GFR and/or in the presence of other risks for progression.
- Avoid nephrotoxic drugs in people with CKD wherever possible.

**ACE inhibitors and ARBs:**
- Do not offer a combination of renin-angiotensin system antagonists (ACE-I + ARB dual therapy) to adults with CKD.
- Single-agent ACE-I or ARB use is appropriate with monitoring.
- Monitor serum potassium and eGFR within 1-2 weeks of starting or changing dose.
- A rise in creatinine of up to 30% after starting ACE-I/ARB is acceptable; greater rises warrant investigation.

**SGLT2 inhibitors for renoprotection (§1.3.16):**
- Offer dapagliflozin 10mg to adults with CKD if eGFR ≥25 mL/min/1.73m² and urine ACR ≥22.6 mg/mmol.
- Can be initiated at eGFR ≥25; do not stop if eGFR falls below this.

### 1.4 CKD Staging (eGFR thresholds)

| Stage | eGFR (mL/min/1.73m²) | Description |
|-------|----------------------|-------------|
| 1 | ≥90 | Normal or high (with other markers of kidney damage) |
| 2 | 60-89 | Mildly decreased |
| 3a | 45-59 | Mildly to moderately decreased |
| 3b | 30-44 | Moderately to severely decreased |
| 4 | 15-29 | Severely decreased |
| 5 | <15 | Kidney failure |

## 1.5 Referral Criteria (§1.5.5)

Refer adults with CKD for specialist assessment if ANY of the following:

1. **5-year risk of needing renal replacement therapy >5%** (using 4-variable Kidney Failure Risk Equation)
2. **ACR ≥70 mg/mmol** (unless known to be caused by diabetes already under specialist management)
3. **Sustained decrease in eGFR of 25% or more within 12 months**
4. **Sustained decrease in eGFR of 15 mL/min/1.73m² or more per year**
5. **eGFR <30** without a known cause
6. **Hypertension that remains poorly controlled** despite 4+ antihypertensive agents
7. **Known or suspected rare or genetic causes** of CKD
8. **Suspected renal artery stenosis**

## Monitoring After Medication Changes

- After starting or changing ACE-I/ARB: recheck eGFR and potassium at 1-2 weeks
- After any nephrotoxic exposure: recheck eGFR at 1 week
- Routine monitoring in CKD 3b: every 6 months minimum
- In rapidly declining eGFR: every 1-3 months until stable
