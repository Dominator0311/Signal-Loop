# NICE NG226 — Osteoarthritis in Over 16s: Diagnosis and Management

Source: National Institute for Health and Care Excellence (2022)
URL: https://www.nice.org.uk/guidance/ng226
Replaces: CG177

## Pharmacological Management of OA Pain

### Analgesic Ladder (in order of recommendation)

**First-line: Topical NSAIDs**
- Offer a topical NSAID to people with knee osteoarthritis (§1.4.2)
- Consider a topical NSAID for osteoarthritis affecting other joints (§1.4.3)
- Minimal systemic absorption — does NOT carry the same renal, cardiovascular, or GI risk as oral NSAIDs
- Appropriate even in patients with CKD (topical diclofenac gel is safe)

**Second-line: Oral NSAIDs (with gastroprotection)**
- Consider an oral NSAID for people with OA when topical options are insufficient (§1.4.4)
- When prescribing oral NSAIDs, take into account potential gastrointestinal, renal, liver, and cardiovascular toxicity
- Consider risk factors: age, pregnancy, current medication, and comorbidities
- ALWAYS co-prescribe gastroprotection (PPI) with oral NSAIDs

**Paracetamol:**
- Do NOT routinely offer paracetamol as first-line for OA (§1.4.5)
- Evidence of benefit is weak
- HOWEVER: paracetamol remains appropriate as an alternative when NSAIDs are contraindicated (e.g., in CKD)
- Safe in renal impairment (no nephrotoxicity)
- Maximum 4g/day; caution with hepatic impairment or chronic alcohol use

### When NOT to Use Oral NSAIDs

Oral NSAIDs should be AVOIDED or used with extreme caution in:
- CKD stage 3b or worse (eGFR <45) — NICE NG203 contraindication
- Concurrent ACE-I/ARB + diuretic (triple-whammy AKI risk)
- Age ≥65 with multiple comorbidities (Beers criteria)
- Heart failure
- History of GI bleeding or peptic ulcer disease
- Concurrent anticoagulant therapy
- Concurrent methotrexate or lithium

### Application to Demo (Margaret):
Margaret has OA bilateral knees (the presenting complaint). She CANNOT receive oral NSAIDs because:
1. eGFR 42 (CKD 3b) — contraindicated per NICE NG203
2. On lisinopril + furosemide — triple-whammy risk
3. Age 72 — Beers criteria

Appropriate alternatives for Margaret:
1. Paracetamol 1g QDS (safe, no renal risk, weak evidence but pragmatic)
2. Topical diclofenac gel (first-line per NG226, minimal systemic absorption)
3. Non-pharmacological: physiotherapy, weight management, exercise programme
