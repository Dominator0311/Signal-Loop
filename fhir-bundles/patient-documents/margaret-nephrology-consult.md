## Clinical Question

Sustained eGFR decline from 58 to 42 over 5 months in context of ACE-I and diuretic use.

## History

72F with T2DM (10 years), HTN (15 years), now CKD 3b. On lisinopril 10mg OD and furosemide 40mg OD. Metformin reportedly stopped 3 months ago (GI intolerance). Simvastatin 20mg nocte.

**Rate of decline:** ~3 points/month. This is faster than expected for diabetic nephropathy alone.

## Examination

## Investigations

- Urine ACR: 12.3 mg/mmol (moderately increased)
- Renal US: Normal sized kidneys, no obstruction, no stones. Mild increased cortical echogenicity consistent with CKD.

## Assessment

CKD stage 3b with rapid progression. Likely multifactorial: diabetic nephropathy + haemodynamic effect of ACE-I + diuretic in the context of possible volume depletion.

## Recommendations

1. **Stop lisinopril.** The ACE-I may be contributing to haemodynamic decline in this context. **Switch to irbesartan 150mg OD** — ARBs are preferred in diabetic nephropathy with proteinuria and may be better tolerated haemodynamically.

2. **Reduce furosemide to 20mg OD** if BP tolerates — consider volume depletion as a contributor.

3. **Recheck eGFR and electrolytes at 6 weeks** to confirm stabilisation.

4. **Absolutely no NSAIDs** — lifelong avoidance given CKD stage and prior triple-whammy risk pattern.

5. **Consider SGLT2 inhibitor** (dapagliflozin 10mg) for renoprotection once eGFR stabilises (NICE NG203 §1.3.16).

6. **Review in my clinic in 3 months** with repeat renal function and ACR.
